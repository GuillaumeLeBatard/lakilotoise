Read me

"La kiloToise" consists in a solo bike challenge which is performed on open roads in a moutain environment. Riders commit their own responsability in this challenge. Pay attention to traffic regulations, as those can be modified upon circumstances, because ofweather conditions or seasonal road works.
In pastoral areas, do not disturb the work of sheperds as well as cattles and wildlife.
For the sake of all participants, it is strongly recommended to start with a bike in a good condition fitted with bright and long-lasting lights, as well as the adequate bikepacking gear. Also, you won't regret a solid training prior to the event!

La kiloToise, the challenge of one day... and one night

Most of the highest roads in the Pyrenees that can be climbed with a road bike orbit around the "Pic du Midi" observatory. The goal of the challenge is to join the summits of all the climbs exceeding one kiloToise in altitude, and to finish this in less than one day! This achievement requires not less than 8 climbs along 320km and 10500m of elevation (5400 toises). You will get such a nice experience of the Gravity!

(*) 1 kiloToise = 1949 meters

In only one day?

Right, the definition of the day can vary from the appr. 24 hours during which the Earth makes a full revolution though. That would be much too restrictive. On the contrary, it is allowed to choose the duration of the day from a self-selected planet. For instance, on Europe, one of Jupiter's 79 moons, one revolution lasts more than 3 days on Earth.
Taking advantage of your own definition of the day, the challenge can be tailored whether it is attempted in solo or with friends, with a tandem, an e-bike, and whatever your level of training. Finishing in less than 24 hours is already a strong accomplishment, probably reserved to the most endurant cyclists.

What's the date?

This is an ITT challenge. As such, the rider is free to set the date at its best convenience. However, the most elevated roads usually remain closed between October and early June. Snowfalls in the late summer are not unusual. In addition, be careful to sudden changes of weather conditions that are likely to happen in the mountains. Take the information prior to your attempt. For cyclists who intend to brave the night, ideally try to get the spotlight of the full moon along your route to enjoy the scenery.

Itineraries

What are the checkpoints? Those which culminate above one kiloToise are: col du Tourmalet with both western-side and eastern-side (this is the only way to answer the still undecided question of the most difficult way to the top), col des Tentes in Cirque de Gavarnie, Cirque de Troumouse, Col de Portet, Cap-de-Long lake and Aubert/Aumar lakes in Néouvielle National Park. Alike some planets that can't be dissociated from their satellites, col d’Aspin and Hourquette d’Ancizan must be added to the list. And so, the route oscillates from well preserved valleys to some of the most epic Tour de France climbs, never far way from the telescops of the "Pic du Midi" observatory.

This is not a race (sic)

At most this challenge is a journey through some of the most spectacular places in the Pyrenees mountains. In the meantime, the mind and the body will have to face a daunting prospect, while the legs will beg for mercy in the steepest climbs, the oxygen atoms become scarce, the temperature skyrockets and drops as quickly when the sun hides below the horizon, etc, etc. No need for a final ranking or a reward, this short and intense travel will tell you the answer : on which planet do you stand? And from there let you target another star!

This format of cycling events is not new, for instance, in the Pyrenees with les Barons du Soulor-Aubisque or l'Ariègeoise perpétuelle, in the Alps with les 7 majeurs, or others like the Everesting Challenge, cyclosportives, brevets... There is already a large panel that worth looking at!

Info

  • Weather

    Check the weather conditions before the attempt. In the mountains, violent thunderstorms can develop. Don't be surprised to experience some snowfalls throughout the summer at these altitudes.
  • Road

    Make sure that mountain roads are opened and in good condition. Some climbs are subject to seasonal or temporary closures.
  • Gear

    Obviously, the machine must be fully checked before leaving, especially tires/tubes and brakes must be in perfect condition. No one enjoys to give-up because of a mechanical failure or a missing tube. Bring a repair kit. Save your legs by selecting the right gearing taking into account the cumulated fatigue and the steep climbs.

  • Safety

    To see and to be visible, here are the priorities. This means carrying at least front and rear lights and a high-visibility jacket. Other must-have items: a helmet, a warm cycling set, a rain jacket, gloves, a phone, money, ID, a GPS or a map.
  • Care about the environment

    Carry your garbage or leave them in "poubelles"/thrash cans. Moreover, the roads bring you accross the Pyrenees National Park (including areas registered at UNESCO) where a specific regulationapplies.
  • Bivouac

    Camping / Bivouac is restricted inside the Pyrenees National Park. In particular, it is prohibited at less than one-hour walking distance to the nearest road.
  • Wildlife

    Be careful of cattles, sheperd dogs, marmots or any other cute animals that may occupy or cross the roads and become a hazard if met in the darkness, under tunnels/shelters or in a descent.
  • Refueling

    Always favor drinking water from fountains or public toilets and avoid rivers or small streams to fill-in the bidons. Open the eyes, you will easily find some water in most towns, as well as food supply (but bakeries or supermarkets are not opened at any time of the day) and a place to sleep or a shelter if the storm is on your heels.
